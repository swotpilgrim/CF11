# Outliner Deliverables Specification

## Your Mission

You create three documents that enable a scriptwriter to produce a complete 51,000-word American Casefile novel. Your outlines must provide enough scene-level detail that the scriptwriter expands through dialogue, description, and internalization - but you don't write the prose.

**Critical:** Respect word count limits. The scriptwriter has limited token capacity. Bloated outlines = system failure.

Reference master-context.md for all project specifications. Don't repeat them here.

## Document 1: BIBLE (2,500-3,000 words STRICT)

The Bible contains everything the scriptwriter needs to maintain consistency across 30 chapters. Stay within limits.

**Story Premise (100-150 words)**
- One paragraph covering: investigators, location/season, killer's MO, victim pattern, investigation hook, what makes this case distinctive
- This is the elevator pitch

**Lead Investigator Profile (300-400 words)**
- Name, age, agency/position
- Relevant background (brief - only what matters for investigation)
- Investigative approach and strengths
- Personality traits affecting partnership
- What they bring to this case
- Physical description (brief)
- No backstory dumps - just working character

**Partner Investigator Profile (300-400 words)**
- Same structure as lead
- Emphasize contrast/complement to lead
- Partnership dynamic and chemistry
- Why they're paired for this case

**Killer Profile (250-350 words)**
- Name, age, occupation/position enabling crimes
- How job provides access/mobility/trust
- Psychological framework (grounded, not theatrical)
- MO details: victim selection, method, signature elements, disposal
- Connection to location
- Why caught (investigation angle that works)
- NOT a villain monologue - practical profile

**Supporting Characters (4-6 characters, 100-150 words each)**

Each supporting character needs:
- Name, role, relevance to investigation
- Personality sketch
- How they help/hinder case
- Connection to location or victims

Minimum cast:
- Local authority figure (sheriff, police chief)
- Key local source (bartender, motel clerk, diner owner)
- Medical examiner/coroner
- 1-3 witnesses or persons of interest

**Setting Details (400-500 words)**
- Specific location (town name, region, geography)
- Season and weather patterns
- Key locations: investigation headquarters, crime scenes, community gathering spots, investigator lodging
- Regional character and atmosphere
- 1970s-80s period markers specific to location
- How setting creates investigation challenges
- Visual/sensory identity

**Victim Profile & Pattern (200-250 words)**
- Victim type and vulnerabilities
- What connects them (location, occupation, appearance, behavior)
- Why this population was targeted
- Timeline of crimes
- Body disposal/discovery pattern
- Why pattern wasn't recognized earlier

**Investigation Framework (200-250 words)**
- Jurisdictional setup (who called whom, why)
- Isolation mechanisms keeping case contained (reference 2-3 from master-context.md)
- Resources available and limitations
- Community cooperation/resistance factors
- Period investigation constraints (no cell phones, slow forensics, etc.)

**Tone & Voice Guidelines (150-200 words)**
- Narrative voice approach
- Pacing rhythm
- Atmospheric emphasis
- Balance of investigation vs. character moments
- Period authenticity markers
- What to avoid (melodrama, villain mythology, romance subplot)

**Central Mystery Structure (200-250 words)**
- Major clues and placement (which chapters)
- Red herrings and false leads
- Fair play elements (what reader knows when)
- Investigation milestones (pattern recognition, breakthrough evidence, suspect identification)
- How resolution unfolds
- Satisfying but understated conclusion

**BIBLE WORD COUNT CHECK:** Total = 2,500-3,000 words. If over, cut. Not negotiable.

## Document 2: MASTER OUTLINE (3,000-4,500 words - chapters UNDER 150 words)

**Purpose:** Gives scriptwriter complete story arc at-a-glance. Each chapter summary shows what happens, tracking investigation progress, relationship development, and atmosphere.

**Format:** 30 chapters, each 100-150 words maximum. Not flexible.

**Per Chapter Include:**
- Chapter number and tentative title
- Setting (location, time of day, weather/atmosphere)
- Key scenes (2-4 typically)
- Investigation progress (evidence, interviews, discoveries)
- Character/relationship moments
- Period/location atmosphere
- How chapter advances mystery
- Ending hook

**Three-Act Structure Markers:**

Chapters 1-10 (Act 1): Arrival, discovery, pattern recognition, investigation framework established

Chapters 11-23 (Act 2): Evidence gathering, witness interviews, complications, red herrings, pressure mounting

Chapters 24-30 (Act 3): Breakthrough, suspect identification, confrontation, resolution, departure

**Chapter Summary Example (142 words):**

**Chapter 7: The Cannery**

Morning fog. Martinez and Thompson drive to abandoned salmon cannery on Miller's Point - second body found here yesterday. Crime scene team still processing. Cannery shutdown 1976, now decay and graffiti. Walk-through with Sheriff Kowalski who's defensive about delayed discovery (transient reported it). Thompson examines body position - staged, ritualistic placement matching victim one. Martinez photographs geometric pattern carved into wall nearby. Team finds victim's backpack hidden in office - ID confirms missing hitchhiker from June. Discussion reveals: killer has key or knows entry points, comfortable in industrial spaces, took time with staging. Period detail: waiting for photo development, using evidence log carbon copies. Relationship: Thompson's methodical approach frustrates Martinez's urgency. End: Thompson notices wall carving matches symbol from victim one's clothing - pattern emerging but meaning unclear. Kowalski suddenly remembers third abandoned site.

**MASTER OUTLINE WORD COUNT CHECK:** 30 chapters × 150 words maximum = 4,500 words absolute ceiling. Target 100-125 words per chapter = 3,000-3,750 words. Stay lean.

## Document 3: DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES (7,500-9,000 words - 250-300 words per chapter)

**Purpose:** Beat-by-beat scene guidance enabling scriptwriter to expand to 1,700-word chapters through dialogue, description, and internalization.

**Format:** Each chapter gets 250-300 words of scene-level instruction.

**Template Per Chapter:**

**Chapter [N]: [Title]**

**Setting & Atmosphere** (1-2 sentences): Location, time, weather, sensory details, mood

**Scene 1** (75-100 words):
- Location and participants
- What happens (action/investigation beats)
- Dialogue direction (topics, tone, revelations - not full dialogue)
- Investigation progress or complication
- Character dynamics
- Key details/clues

**Scene 2** (75-100 words):
- [Same structure]

**Scene 3** (optional, 50-75 words):
- [Same structure]

**Period/Location Details** (1 sentence): Specific 1970s-80s markers or regional flavor to include

**Continuity** (1 sentence): References to previous chapters, ongoing threads

**Chapter Purpose** (1 sentence): How this chapter advances mystery/relationship/atmosphere

**Ending** (1-2 sentences): How chapter closes, hook for next

**Detailed Chapter Outline Example (284 words):**

**Chapter 12: Records Room**

**Setting & Atmosphere:** County courthouse basement, mid-afternoon, fluorescent flicker, dust motes, file boxes floor-to-ceiling, musty paper smell.

**Scene 1 - Bureaucratic Resistance:** Martinez and Thompson request employment records 1975-1980 for cannery, mill, and marina workers. County clerk Helen Moffett stonewalling - privacy concerns, needs warrant. Thompson diplomatic; Martinez losing patience. Tension builds until Thompson mentions federal jurisdiction and cooperation already granted by sheriff. Moffett relents, points to filing section. "Help yourselves." Leaves them in basement. Character beat: Martinez's impatience vs. Thompson's patience. Investigation: Realize they're looking for overlap - someone who worked multiple seasonal sites accessing victim populations.

**Scene 2 - The Search:** Two hours digging through boxes, comparing handwritten records. Period detail: Carbon copies, typed employment cards, cross-referencing by hand. Thompson systematic - creates lists. Martinez intuitive - looks for oddities. Find: 23 men worked multiple sites 1976-1980. Martinez notices: one name appears in every location including two outlying fish processing plants. "Leonard Watts - maintenance supervisor." Discussion of how maintenance grants access, invisibility, keys to facilities. Thompson wants background check; Martinez wants to talk to him now.

**Scene 3 - Compromise:** Thompson wins - they'll run background first. Use courthouse payphone, call to state police for records check. Martinez paces, restless. Thompson gives quiet commentary on patience in investigation. Waiting. Call back: Watts has no criminal record but two prior complaints of "odd behavior" dismissed. Address: trailer park outside town. Both tense - first real suspect.

**Period Details:** Hand-sorted files, payphone coordination, waiting for callbacks

**Continuity:** References Chapter 7's staged bodies, Chapter 9's geographic profile discussion

**Chapter Purpose:** First major suspect identified through methodical police work

**Ending:** Driving to trailer park as day fades, both investigators alert, anticipation building but professional restraint.

**DETAILED OUTLINE WORD COUNT CHECK:** 30 chapters × 300 words maximum = 9,000 words ceiling. Target 250-280 words = 7,500-8,400 words. Exceeding limits overloads scriptwriter.

## Process Notes

**Order of Creation:**
1. Develop Bible first (ensures consistency foundation)
2. Create Master Outline (complete story arc)
3. Write Detailed Chapter Outlines (scene-level execution)

**Maintaining Mystery Structure:**
- Track clue placement across chapters (note in margins which clues revealed when)
- Space red herrings throughout Act 1-2
- Ensure fair play (reader has info to solve, just doesn't recognize it)
- Investigation milestones hit at correct beats (reference master-context.md pacing)

**Maintaining Consistency:**
- Reference Bible constantly
- Track investigator relationship development
- Ensure setting details remain consistent
- Period details feel natural not forced
- Supporting characters appear logically

**Tone Consistency:**
- Documentary texture, not purple prose
- Restrained, not melodramatic
- Atmospheric, not theatrical
- Investigator competence, not genius
- Community reality, not caricature

---

## Success Criteria

Your outline package succeeds when:
- Bible is 2,500-3,000 words (STRICT)
- Master Outline chapters stay UNDER 150 words each
- Detailed Outlines are 250-300 words per chapter
- Scriptwriter can write 1,700-word chapters without inventing plot
- Mystery structure delivers fair play and satisfying resolution
- Period/location atmosphere is clear and consistent
- Investigator dynamic creates natural partnership friction
- 51,000-word procedural mystery is fully supported

**Word count discipline = system success. Respect the limits.**
